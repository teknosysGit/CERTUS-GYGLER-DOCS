# Safety

This section describes the essential safety requirements and provides information on {{ variables.product.name.en }}'s safe operation.

## Intended Use

The unit is intended for the following applications and uses:

* Fluid dispensing within dosage ranges starting from 50 nl for pharmacological, cytological, toxicological, chemical, biochemical, or genetical process and assay development
* ANSI-compliant well plate dispensing (low throughput dispensing)
* Use on an indoor laboratory bench
* Maximum temperature of fluids to be processed: 60°C

Any other or excessive use is deemed non-intended use. The manufacturer cannot be held liable for any damage that may be caused by it.

The intended use also includes the following:

* Compliance with all the instructions contained in this user manual.

## Non-Intended Use

The following examples illustrate non-intended use (this list is not exhaustive):

* Installation, start-up, operation, and maintenance without prior instruction
* Operation outside the limits of technical specifications and beyond allowable environmental conditions
* Deactivation of safety devices
* Failure to comply with transportation, storage, installation, start-up, operation, maintenance, and repair instructions in the user manual
* Any alterations, modifications, or changes to the unit without the manufacturer's written approval
* Changes to the software without the manufacturer's written approval
* Improper repairs
* Use of incompatible materials
* Use of incompatible or damaged parts

!!! note
    All warranty and liability claims are excluded if damage or injury is attributable to non-intended use.

## Personnel Qualifications

All work on and involving the unit may only be performed by qualified and trained laboratory personnel who have read and understood this user manual.

The end user of the unit is responsible for:

- Selection, instruction, and training of personnel
- Continuous monitoring of work safety

### Requirements

- All work should only be performed by qualified laboratory personnel
- Insufficiently qualified persons should be kept away from the workspace
- No children or persons whose reactions might be affected (for example, by drugs, alcohol, or medications) should stay within the operating range of the unit

## Basic Safety Regulations

### Regulations and Work Safety

* The operation of the unit is subject to local legislation as well as occupational safety and accident prevention regulations
* All safety and protection devices should be checked whenever the unit is started up
* Procedures that might compromise the safety of the unit should be refrained from
* Implementation and monitoring of safety measures are the end user's responsibility

### General Inspections and Maintenance

All inspection and maintenance instructions contained in this user manual should be followed.

### Alterations

Alterations (including all additions and conversions, the use of non-original parts, and changes to software) may only be made upon prior consultation with and written approval from the manufacturer.

Where necessary, any changes required to this user manual should only be made by the manufacturer and communicated to the end user of the unit.

### Power Supply

The connection of power supply is subject to the power supply ratings and tolerances as well as the power input provided in the Technical Specifications section.

### Spare Parts

To ensure safe operation, only spare parts specified by the manufacturer should be used. Otherwise, all warranty and liability claims against the manufacturer expire.

### Fluid Media

Some fluid media can be hazardous. The intensity of the possible hazard potential, characteristics, and type of test media can be derived from the following data sources:

* Hazard assessment and properties of fluids used
* Safety data sheets of fluids used
* Labeling of fluids used
* Databases of chemical compounds
* System for the classification and identification of chemicals (Globally Harmonized System of Classification, Labeling and Packaging of Chemicals)
* National and local laws and information documents

#### Handling Requirements

Handling of test media should be adjusted depending on their properties and hazard potential as well as in accordance with related regulations. This applies to:

* Storage and transportation
* Use
* Combination and contact with various materials
* Disposal

!!! note
    Applicable environmental protection and water pollution control requirements should be complied with.

### Personal Protective Equipment

Personal protective equipment should be worn, including:

* **Safety goggles** (absolutely mandatory)
* Protective gloves and protective clothing (where appropriate)

The exact type, components, and materials of protective equipment should be defined by the end user depending on the hazard.

#### Applicable Regulations

At least the following regulations and instructions should be observed:

* Hazard assessment and properties of materials used
* End user's regulations
* Safety data sheets of materials used
* Labeling of materials used
* Good Laboratory Practice (GLP) regulations
* Local and national legislation, such as inspection plans and operating instructions

### Dealing with Malfunctions

All work to fix malfunctions should only be performed during downtimes. If a malfunction cannot be properly addressed by operators using simple methods, seek assistance from qualified maintenance and service personnel.

### Cleaning, Disinfection, and Sterilization

!!! note
    Adequate cleaning adapted to local conditions and, where appropriate, disinfection and sterilization of all work surfaces and objects used are required for the proper functioning of all processes.

It is the responsibility of the end user or their assignees to clearly and actually define and organize cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization processes.

### Disposal

National and local waste disposal laws and regulations should be observed. Strictly defined instructions on general waste separation as well as detailed definitions regarding the handling and disposal of hazardous waste are usually in place.

!!! important
    Hazardous and toxic substances should be disposed of as special waste.

For this purpose, the attached decontamination declaration has to be completed (see [Decontamination Declaration](#decontamination-declaration)).

## Responsibilities

### Responsibilities of the End User

To the outside world, the "end user" is any natural or legal person who has decisive economic power over the technical operation of the unit. The end user may enter into a contract to assign its obligations to any person in charge.

Unless they contradict any local law or regulation, the end user's responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

* Preventing hazards
* Keeping equipment safe
* Arranging training for users
* Arranging appropriate and qualified tests
* Arranging maintenance
* Arranging safety and measurement inspections (where necessary)
* Arranging disinfection and sterilization (where appropriate)
* Arranging documentation
* Arranging functional tests
* Arranging briefings
* Reporting incidents

### Responsibilities of Operating Personnel

Operating personnel's responsibilities include:

* Reading and understanding this user manual
* Using the unit only when it is in good working condition
* Using and taking care of required protective equipment
* Ensuring cleanliness and clarity around the unit

### Responsibilities of Maintenance Personnel

Servicing or maintenance is ensured by your distributor or properly trained personnel.

## First Aid in Case of Accident

Measures to be taken in case of accident are defined and implemented by the end user. The end user has to make sure that personnel is regularly trained in this respect.

### Training Areas

Such training covers the following areas:

* First aid
* Emergency call (arranging transportation, calling a doctor)
* In case of accident, situation recognition and emergency measures, including self-protection:
  * General (bone fractures, bleeding, respiratory/cardiac arrest, etc.)
  * Caused by electric shock
  * Caused by handling hazardous substances
  * Caused by burns and scalds
  * Caused by chemical burns
